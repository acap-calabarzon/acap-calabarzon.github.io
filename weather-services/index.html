Weather Systems that May Affect the Region
  • Southwest Monsoon: Also known as Habagat in the Philippines, this is a seasonal wind that brings heavy rains from the southwest, typically from June to September.
  • Easterlies: Winds that blow from the east, often bringing warm and humid air, and can influence weather patterns, especially during the dry season.
  • Ridge of High Pressure Area: An elongated area of high pressure that can lead to fair weather and clear skies, often extending from a high-pressure system.
  • Localized Thunderstorms: Short-lived thunderstorms that develop in a specific area, often producing heavy rain, lightning, and gusty winds.
  • Shearline: A weather phenomenon where two air masses meet, often leading to the development of clouds and precipitation.
  • Frontal Systems: Boundaries between two air masses with different temperatures and humidity, often leading to precipitation.
  • ITCZ (Intertropical Convergence Zone): A region near the equator where trade winds from the Northern and Southern Hemispheres meet, causing frequent thunderstorms and heavy rainfall.
  • LPA (Low Pressure Area): A region where the atmospheric pressure is lower than its surroundings, often leading to cloud formation and precipitation, and can develop into tropical cyclones.
  • Tropical Cyclones: Intense circular storms that originate over warm tropical oceans, characterized by strong winds, heavy rain, and can cause significant damage.
  • Northeast Monsoon: Also known as Amihan, this is a seasonal wind from the northeast, bringing cooler and drier air, typically from November to early March.
